🔴 Agent Decision Logic — D · SAQ D Merchant Risk Reduction Advisory
When the merchant's profile results in SAQ D — proactively advise risk reduction
🔴 SAQ D is the highest-risk and most complex SAQ type. It typically applies when a merchant stores cardholder data electronically, has a mixed payment environment, or doesn't qualify for a simpler SAQ. Agents should proactively advise these merchants on how to reduce their compliance scope.
SCRIPT — Risk Reduction Conversation:
🎙 Agent
"I can see from the profile that your account has been assigned SAQ D, which is our most detailed compliance questionnaire. This typically means the system has identified that your business environment is more complex — for example, if you're storing card data or using multiple payment methods. The good news is there are steps you can take that could reduce your compliance requirements significantly."
Practical Risk Reduction Suggestions — Keep Simple:
TIP 1
Stop storing card data — if the merchant stores full PANs or CVV after authorisation, advise them to stop immediately. Once storage is eliminated, they may qualify for a simpler SAQ.
TIP 2
Use a redirect / hosted payment page — switching to a third-party checkout (e.g. PayPal, Stripe redirect) means card data never touches their server, potentially reducing scope to SAQ A.
TIP 3
Segment your network — isolating the payment system from the rest of the business network reduces PCI DSS scope. Advise them to speak to their IT provider about this.
TIP 4
Use a validated P2PE solution — if they accept card present payments, using a PCI-validated P2PE device encrypts data at point of interaction, potentially reducing scope to SAQ P2PE.
ℹ️ Escalation note: If the merchant is confused about why they received SAQ D, or believes it is incorrect — check their profile answers carefully. If the SAQ assignment appears wrong, escalate to your team lead or a senior agent. Do not change the SAQ type without authorisation.

§6a — Steps to Schedule the Scan

IP Address — 5 Steps
  1. To run the scan, we will need to know the IP address at your business. Are you at your business now?
  2. Do you have access to a device or computer that is connected to the same internet as your terminal?
  3. Once on that computer/device, please open an internet browser (Chrome, Internet Explorer, Firefox, Safari etc.) and type in www.whatismyip.com. This will bring up your public IP address as a series of numbers and dots.
  4. Do you have a load balancer? (If yes: "A load balancer is a component on a network that evenly distributes traffic across multiple servers so that they do not overload and crash. Example: A concert or sporting event website would have a load balancer in place to ensure the website won't crash under heavy traffic.")
  5. Now, I will just need your verbal confirmation that you are allowing our IP address to scan yours for this External Vulnerability scan?
Web Domain — 3 Steps
  1. To run the scan, we will need your company's web domain name (e.g. www.test.com)
  2. Do you have a load balancer? (If yes: "A load balancer is a component on a network that evenly distributes traffic across multiple servers so that they do not overload and crash.")

SAQ Type Reference
Self-Assessment Questionnaire types — who they apply to, scan requirements, and key characteristics
ℹ️ SAQ type is determined during the Business Profile. Always read SAQ questions from your script — not from the screen. If the merchant has a Special Note, check the guidance link on the portal first.
SAQ A
Card-Not-Present / Fully Outsourced
Applies to
E-commerce merchants; all cardholder data functions fully outsourced to validated third parties (iFrame, redirect). MOTO merchants with fully outsourced processing.
Scan
Required (domain scan if iFrame/redirect used) · Not required for Pay by Link only
Storage
No electronic storage, processing, or transmission of cardholder data on merchant systems
Key note
Simplest SAQ. Merchant's website does not receive cardholder data. Pay by Link = no scan; iFrame/redirect = domain scan needed.
SAQ A-EP
E-Commerce — Partially Outsourced (JS / Silent Post)
Applies to
E-commerce merchants whose website hosts or controls payment page elements via JavaScript or Direct Post / Silent Post. Card data goes browser → PSP directly.
Scan
Required — domain/URL scan (ASV). Website is internet-facing and in scope.
Storage
No CHD on merchant servers, but merchant's code can influence the payment page
Key note
Higher risk than SAQ A. Merchant controls how card data is redirected; merchant's site is never fully hands-off. Silent Post / JS integration = A-EP, not A.
SAQ D
High Risk / Composite / Multiple Methods
Applies to
High Risk: Merchant stores CHD, accepts card details by email/fax, records calls with card data, uses POS batch files with full PAN, no POI device on mobile, or integrated non-secure payment page. Composite: Multiple payment methods under same MID (e.g. website + terminal).
Scan
Required — always for High Risk · Depends on setups included for Composite
Storage
High Risk: merchant systems directly handle card data. Most complex SAQ — no outsourcing.
Key note
ISP must be explained for Composite. SAQ D-SP (service providers) — Helpdesk does NOT support. ROC is a full QSA audit for Level 1 merchants.
SAQ B
Imprint / Standalone Terminal (Phone/ISDN)
Applies to
Merchants using imprint machines only, or standalone dial-out terminals connected via phoneline / ISDN. Face-to-face and MOTO transactions.
Scan
Not required — terminal not connected to internet
Storage
No electronic cardholder data storage
Key note
Standalone terminal – Phoneline. Not connected to any other systems or internet.
SAQ B-IP
Standalone IP-Connected Terminal — Two Versions
Applies to
Merchants using standalone PTS-approved terminals connected via IP. Terminal not connected to other merchant systems. Face-to-face and MOTO.
Scan
Required (quarterly IP scan) if Internet/Wi-Fi/broadband · Not required if GPRS/SIM — terminal bypasses merchant's internet
Storage
No electronic cardholder data storage
Key note
Two versions: Internet (public IPv4 scan required) vs GPRS/SIM (no scan — SIM connects direct to processor). PCI SSC PTS-approved device required for both.
SAQ C
Mobile / Tablet with Card Reader (POI device)
Applies to
Merchants using a mobile or tablet with a physical card reader (POI device) and a payment app. Face-to-face only. No card data storage.
Scan
Not required
Storage
No electronic cardholder data storage
Key note
MUST have a POI (Point of Interaction) card reader device — if no POI device attached to mobile, merchant becomes SAQ D High Risk.
SAQ C
iPOS / ePOS — POS Software on Computer or Tablet
Applies to
Merchants using POS software on a computer or tablet (e.g. Clover, EPOS/IPOS systems). Face-to-face or MOTO. No card data storage.
Scan
Required — public IP scan (current public IP must be obtained; each location scanned; IPv6 invalid)
Storage
No electronic cardholder data storage
Key note
Cannot use batch settlement files containing full card numbers. Clover, IPOS/EPOS systems fall here.
SAQ C-VT
Virtual Terminal — Isolated Computer
Applies to
Merchants who process cardholder data only via a virtual terminal on an isolated, dedicated computer. Typically single admin user.
Scan
Not required
Storage
No electronic cardholder data storage
Key note
Virtual Terminal setup. Computer used exclusively for payment processing — no other business functions.
SAQ P2PE
Point-to-Point Encryption Hardware Terminal
Applies to
Merchants using hardware payment terminals within a validated P2PE solution. Cardholder data is encrypted at point of interaction.
Scan
Not required
Storage
No electronic cardholder data storage by merchant
Key note
IPOS/EPOS with P2PE solution. Significantly reduced scope due to encryption. Fewest requirements of all SAQ types.
SAQ SPOC
Secure PIN on COTS Device (Smartphone/Tablet + SCRP)
Applies to
Merchants accepting EMV chip + PIN on a COTS device (smartphone/tablet) using a PCI-validated SPoC solution with an approved SCRP card reader (RFID — supports Tap to Pay). Face-to-face and MOTO.
Scan
Not required — SPoC security model de-scopes scanning
Storage
No cardholder data stored on phone. All processing outsourced to SPoC solution provider.
Key note
PIN entered on mobile via validated SPoC app. Target: mobile businesses, home service providers, on-the-go merchants. ⚠ Not available for Fiserv / Elavon acquirers.

SAQ SPOC
Software-Based PIN Entry on COTS
PIN on smartphone/tablet · Validated SPOC solution · No scan required · Face-to-face and MOTO
▼
📖 What is SPOC?
SPOC stands for Secure PIN Entry on Commercial Off-The-Shelf (COTS) devices — a PCI-validated solution that allows merchants to accept EMV chip + PIN card-present payments using a standard smartphone or tablet
What is a COTS device? Mass-produced, readily available consumer electronics — not custom-built for payment processing. Examples: smartphones, tablets, standard consumer-grade devices
In a SPoC solution the COTS device works together with: a secure PIN-entry application + a PCI-approved external card reader (SCRP). This ensures cardholder data and PINs are never exposed to the phone's OS or other apps
SCRP (Secured Card Reader PIN device) is an RFID-capable device — supports Tap to Pay (contactless)
🔒 What SPOC Guarantees (5 Core Protections)
1. Secure PIN entry on an untrusted device (smartphone)
2. Cryptographic protection of cardholder data and PINs
3. No storage of cardholder data on the phone
4. Use of PCI-approved hardware readers for reading chip cards
5. Secure communication between card reader ↔ COTS device ↔ payment processor
🎯 Who qualifies for SAQ SPOC?
Merchant accepts PIN payments on COTS devices (smartphones or tablets) using a validated SPoC solution listed on the PCI SSC website
Merchant uses a mobile/tablet with a Secured Card Reader PIN (SCRP) device — RFID device that supports Tap to Pay
Merchant accepts Face-to-Face AND Mail or Telephone (MOTO) transactions
Merchant outsources all payment processing to the SPoC solution provider
PIN is entered on the mobile device via the validated SPoC application
Specifically designed for mobile and micro-merchants — mobile businesses, home service providers, on-the-go merchants
📋 Key Question Areas
Is the SPoC solution being used as specified by the solution provider without modification?
Is the COTS device (phone/tablet) used only for payment acceptance, or is its use restricted appropriately?
Is the SPoC application kept up to date — are solution provider updates applied promptly?
Are the COTS devices and card readers physically secured and accounted for (inventory maintained)?
Are staff trained to identify suspicious behaviour or device interference at the point of payment?
Are cardholder data and PIN data protected — no storage of sensitive authentication data?
⭐ Key Facts
Relatively new SAQ type — introduced to address the growing use of smartphone/tablet-based payment acceptance (tap-to-phone, mPOS era)
No scan required — even though the COTS device is internet-connected, SPoC's security model de-scopes scanning
Key difference from P2PE: SPOC relies on software-based PIN security on a general-purpose device rather than dedicated encrypted hardware
Key difference from SAQ C (Mobile): SPOC uses an external SCRP card reader — SAQ C Mobile also uses a card reader but without the validated SPoC PIN solution
The security model relies on the validated SPoC software isolating PIN entry from the rest of the device's operating environment — so even though the phone is a general-purpose device, card data and PINs are never accessible to other apps or the OS.
⚠️Not available for some acquirers — Fiserv and Elavon do not support SPoC. Always check acquirer compatibility before advising a merchant to use this solution.

PCI DSS Fundamentals
What PCI DSS is · CHD vs SAD · Merchant levels · Card processing stages
▼
What is PCI DSS?
PCI DSS = Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard — a set of technical and operational requirements to protect cardholder data
Mandatory for any business that accepts credit or debit cards — not a law, but created in 2006 by the 5 major card brands
Founded by: Visa, Mastercard, Discover Financial, JCB International, American Express
Goal: reduce payment card fraud and increase merchant awareness of data security
⚠️ PCI DSS is NOT a law — but non-compliance consequences include fines, card replacement fees, forensic investigation costs, brand/reputation damage, and non-compliance charges
Benefits of Compliance
Better protection of customer card data
Avoid financial losses from breach costs and penalties
Increased customer confidence
Protect brand reputation from breach impact
Cardholder Data (CHD) vs Sensitive Authentication Data (SAD)
Cardholder Data (CHD)Sensitive Authentication Data (SAD)
PAN (Primary Account Number)CVV / CVC (3-4 digit security code — CAV2, CID, CVV)
Cardholder nameTrack data (magnetic stripe)
Expiry dateChip data
PIN data
⚠️ SAD (CVV, track data, PIN) must NEVER be stored after authorisation — not even if encrypted
Cardholder Data Environment (CDE)
CDE = all systems, people, processes, and premises involved in storing, processing, or transmitting cardholder data
Example: a local shop's POS terminal, router connected to POS, network switch, and payment application — ALL are CDE
PCI DSS requirements apply only to the CDE — if it touches card data, it's in scope
PAN is ALWAYS in scope — no exceptions
Full track data must never be stored even if encrypted or masked
Other PCI Standards
PA-DSS — Payment Application Data Security Standard (application security)
PCI SSS — Secure Software Standard
P2PE — Point-to-Point Encryption Standard
PTS — PIN Transaction Security

SAQ Types — Detailed Summary
All SAQ types including D Composite, D High Risk, D-SP, ROC · Scan requirements · B vs B-IP comparison
▼
How SAQ Type is Determined — 3 Factors
1. How they accept payments (face-to-face, ecommerce, phone/mail, pay-by-link)
2. What type of payment system they use (terminal, virtual terminal, POS, etc.)
3. Whether they store cardholder data electronically → If YES, automatically gets High-Risk SAQ D
SAQ A — Payment Fully Outsourced
For online/ecommerce & MOTO. Payments fully outsourced to PCI-compliant provider
Website uses iFrame or 3rd-party redirect checkout. Customers enter card on third-party hosted page, never merchant site
Pay by Link — merchant sends customer secure link supplied by PCI-compliant 3rd party — no scan for PBL
Domain scan required if iFrame/redirect is used (EVS IS required)
SAQ A-EP — Payments Partially Outsourced
For ecommerce only. Silent Post/JS creates payment page and transports data to PSP
Merchant website hosts payment page elements or components that influence it — never handles card data directly
Higher risk than SAQ A (due to potential storage). Domain scan/EVS required
⚠️ Key difference: SAQ A = fully sends customer to 3rd party page. SAQ A-EP = JS/Direct Post takes card data from merchant website to processor — riskier
SAQ B — Standalone Phoneline Terminal
Standalone card machine on phone line. Face-to-face or MOTO. No card data storage. No scans needed
SAQ B-IP — Two Versions
Internet (Wi-Fi/broadband) → scan required on Public IP
GPRS/SIM based → scan NOT required (not connected to merchant's internet)
Still standalone machines. Face-to-face or MOTO. No card data storage. Requires PEDs to be PCI SSC PTS-approved
SAQ C-VT — Virtual Terminal
Merchant uses a browser-based Virtual Terminal (web version of a physical CC terminal). Used for MOTO transactions
No storage. No scans needed. Computer must be dedicated to VT only — no other business use
SAQ C (Mobile) — Mobile with Card Reader
Mobile/tablet with card reader + payment app. Face to face. No storage. No scans
⚠️ MUST have a POI (Point of Interaction) card reader device — if no POI device, it's SAQ D High Risk
SAQ C (iPOS/ePOS) — POS Software
Using POS software on computer/tablet (e.g. Clover). Face to face or MOTO. No storage
Scan required — internet is required — on Public IP address
⚠️ Can't use batch settlement files containing full card numbers
SAQ SPoC — Secure PIN on COTS
COTS = Commercial Off-the-Shelf device (standard smartphone or tablet)
Uses a PCI-validated SPoC solution with a secure, PCI-approved card reader. PIN entered on the mobile device
Outsource all payment processing to the SPoC solution provider. No scans
⚠️ Not available for some acquirers (Fiserv/Elavon). For mobile businesses, home service providers, on-the-go merchants
SAQ P2PE — Point-to-Point Encryption
Using PCI-approved P2PE terminal. Strongest encryption — data converted to unreadable format at point of interaction
No scans required. POS setup: a payment terminal or iPOS/ePOS that is a P2PE hardware solution
SAQ D (Composite)
Merchant uses multiple payment methods under same MID — example: website + terminal
Scan depends on what setups are included. ISP must be explained
SAQ type will show up with multiple mentions — e.g. SAQ Type D (A, B)
SAQ D (High Risk)
Assigned if merchant engages in high-risk behaviour: storing cardholder data, accepting CHD through email, recording calls containing card details, integrated (non-secure) payment pages, POS batch files with full PAN, no POI device attached to mobile, fax server sending CHD to email
Scans required. Most complex SAQ. No outsourcing. Merchant systems directly involved with card data
Additional SAQ Types (Agent Info Only)
SAQ D-SP — For service providers handling CHD for merchants. Expert option only. Helpdesk does NOT support
ROC (Report on Compliance) — Full audit done by a QSA. Usually for large Level 1 merchants. Extremely detailed compliance report

What is an External Vulnerability Scan (EVS/ASV)?
A non-intrusive test performed from outside the merchant's network. VikingCloud IS an ASV (Approved Scanning Vendor)
Checks external-facing systems for vulnerabilities that could be exploited
Metaphor: 'Like checking all doors and windows of a house from outside'
Required quarterly (every 90 days) if the merchant's SAQ type requires it
Purpose: identify vulnerabilities in the CDE and ensure secure communication for card data processing
Scan Scheduling — 7-Step Portal Process
1. Click Manage under Be Scan Compliant
2. Confirm merchant is at their business location and has access to a device on the same internet as the terminal
3. On that device, open a browser and go to www.whatismyip.com
4. Ask for the public IP address (IPv4) — a series of numbers and dots — type it into the required box and click Add
5. Change the scan time to one hour forward. Do NOT change the scan date unless required
6. Ask if the merchant uses a Load Balancer (a component that evenly distributes traffic across multiple servers to prevent overload and crashes)
7. Ask for verbal confirmation that the merchant allows our IP address to scan theirs — you cannot click 'Schedule Scan' until merchant agrees
⚠️ Once scheduled: 'The length of time it takes to scan your network may vary. We cannot give an estimated completion time, but you will be notified via email when the scan is complete.'
IP Address Rules (Critical)
Public IP
Required for external scan · Must be IPv4 · Found at whatismyip.com · Assigned by ISP, unique worldwide
Private IP
Used only on local networks · Not scannable externally
Static IP
Never changes · Merchant must whitelist scanner IP ranges
Dynamic IP
Changes periodically (reconnect or ISP action)
Scanner Whitelisting IPs (Critical Detail)
⚠️ Merchant MUST whitelist these IP ranges for VikingCloud scans. Failure to whitelist → scan will fail.
Standard scanner IPs: 64.39.96.0/20 154.59.121.0/24 139.87.112.0/23 New scanner IP (IMPORTANT — for static IP merchants): 34.33.2.64/26

A revalidation occurs when a merchant has previously completed PCI compliance and reaches their yearly renewal point

ACTION GUIDE
Scenario-Based Action Guide
Decision-based troubleshooting for the most common complex situations · Situation → Steps → Key Checks → Resolution path
💡 This guide covers the six most complex or ambiguous scenarios agents encounter. Each block defines the situation, the steps to take, what to check, and how to resolve it — including escalation paths where required.
AG1 "Null and Void" / Website Not Loading Error 🌐 Portal Error ▼
📋 Situation
! Merchant reports they are seeing a "null and void" message, blank page, spinning wheel, or the portal is not loading correctly when they try to access the PCI compliance website.
⚙️ Steps to Take
1Ask the merchant which browser they are currently using. Note the browser name and version if possible.
2Ask them to clear their browser cache and cookies: Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data → Cached images and files + Cookies → Clear data.
3Once cache is cleared, ask them to refresh the page (F5 or Ctrl+R) and try again.
4If the issue persists, ask them to try a different browser (e.g. if they are using Chrome, try Edge or Firefox).
5If still not resolved, ask them to try on a different device (e.g. another computer, tablet, or mobile phone).
6If none of the above work, confirm they are on the correct portal URL for their programme (not a generic search result or bookmark).
🔍 Key Checks
✓Is the merchant on the correct portal URL for their acquirer/programme?
✓Is the portal currently experiencing downtime? (Check internal comms or contact your team lead.)
✓Is the merchant's internet connection working? Ask them to try another website to confirm.
✅ Resolution Path
Most cases resolve with cache clear + correct browser. If the issue persists after trying all three (clear cache → different browser → different device), raise a technical issue ticket noting the merchant's MID, browser used, device type, and exact error message. Advise the merchant you will follow up once the technical team reviews.
AG2 Login Credential Issues — Full Resolution Options 🔑 Credentials ▼
📋 Situation
!Merchant cannot log in, has forgotten credentials, has not received login emails, or their account access is otherwise broken. Multiple resolution paths apply depending on the specific issue.
⚠️ALWAYS check email access first — see Decision Logic C (Login Credential Recovery) in Supp A before proceeding. Never send credentials to an email the merchant cannot access.
⚙️ Resolution Options — Choose the Right Path
A Add a secondary user: If the primary user cannot log in and a second contact exists, add them as a secondary user on the portal under User Management. Ensure identity is verified first.
B Change/update primary email: If the registered email is wrong or inaccessible, update it on the portal before sending any credentials. Confirm new email letter by letter. Re-check on screen before saving.
C Add CC email: If the merchant wants compliance communications copied to an additional address, add a CC email on the portal under the contact settings — confirm address letter by letter.
D Reset credentials (standard flow): Confirm email access → Send Email 1 (username) → Send Email 2 (password reset link) → Advise to check junk/spam → Stay on call or advise them to call back once received.
🔍 Key Checks
✓Has the merchant received the email? Always advise them to check junk/spam before assuming it was not sent.
✓Is the account locked? Check on the portal — if locked, unlock from the User Account section.
✓Does the password meet the requirements? (Min 8 chars, upper, lower, number, special char, no more than 2 repeating in same case.)
✅ Resolution Path
Resolve using the correct path (A–D) based on the merchant's specific situation. After any credential reset — confirm the merchant has received and opened the emails before closing the call. If they cannot receive emails at all → update email first, then resend. If account remains locked after unlock attempt → escalate.
AG3 Scan-Related Issues — Scheduled / Not Set Up / Failed 🔍 Scan ▼
🔀 Decision — Which scan situation applies?
What is the current scan status on the portal?
SCHEDULED
Scan is set up and scheduled. Merchant is waiting for results. → See path A below.
NOT SET UP
Merchant hasn't set up a scan yet, or scan was never configured. → See path B below.
FAILED
Scan has run and returned a fail result. → See path C below (full troubleshooting).
⚙️ Path A — Scan Already Scheduled
1Confirm on the portal: scan is scheduled and the IP address / domain is correctly entered.
2Advise the merchant to keep their router/internet on during the scan window so it can run uninterrupted.
3Let them know they will receive an email with the scan results. For PDS merchants — we will call them when results are in.
!If scan is overdue — flag urgency. Quarterly scans are required. Advise they must not delay or compliance will lapse.
⚙️ Path B — Scan Needs to Be Set Up
1Confirm the SAQ type requires a scan (check Decision Logic A above if unsure).
2Ask: "Are you at your business now? Do you have access to a device on the same internet as your terminal?"
3For IP scan: Guide merchant to www.whatismyip.com to retrieve their public IPv4 address (format: numbers and dots only — IPv6 is invalid).
4For domain scan: Confirm the merchant's website URL (e.g. www.yourbusiness.com).
5Enter the IP / domain on the portal under the scan section. Get verbal confirmation that the merchant authorises the scan.
6Schedule the scan. Advise the merchant the scan runs in the background and will not affect their network.
⚠️ Path C — Failed Scan (Full Troubleshooting)
!Do NOT attempt to interpret or resolve failed scan findings yourself. The Scan team handles this. Your role is to triage and arrange the callback.
1Confirm on the portal that the scan has actually failed (not a "special note" or pending state — see Scenario 8 for special notes).
2Empathise with the merchant: "I can see your scan has failed. I understand this can be confusing — I'm going to arrange for our specialist Scan team to contact you and walk you through exactly what needs to be done."
3Apply the Scanning macro on Zendesk. Fill in the internal note: MID, merchant name, best contact number, best callback time, scan failure details.
4Advise merchant: "Please do not make any changes to your network setup until our team contacts you — any changes could affect the scan results."
!Submit Zendesk ticket as OPEN — not Solved. Scan team will follow up.
✅ Resolution Path
Scheduled: Confirm setup is correct, advise merchant to keep router on, await results.
Not set up: Walk through scan setup on the call using IP/domain steps above.
Failed: Apply Scanning macro → arrange Scan team callback → leave ticket Open → do not attempt to resolve scan failures yourself.
AG4 SAQ D Merchant — High Risk Guidance & Risk Reduction 🔴 High Risk ▼
📋 Situation
!The merchant's profile has resulted in SAQ D — either due to electronic storage of card data, a mixed or complex payment environment, or by not qualifying for a simpler SAQ type. This is the most demanding compliance questionnaire.
⚙️ What to Explain to the Merchant
1Explain the situation clearly: "Your profile results show that your business has been assigned SAQ D — our most comprehensive questionnaire. This is because [state the reason from profile e.g. you store card data / mixed payment methods / etc.]."
2Set expectations: SAQ D covers all 12 PCI DSS requirement areas. It is more detailed and requires more controls. A vulnerability scan is also required.
3Proactively advise risk reduction — use the Decision Logic D block in the Business Profile section (P4) for the full script and practical tips to reduce scope.
4Walk through the SAQ D questions on the portal as you would with any other SAQ — read from your script, do not skip questions, do not answer on behalf of the merchant.
🔍 Key Checks
✓Is the SAQ D assignment correct? Cross-reference the profile answers. If the merchant believes it's wrong → check profile settings and escalate if needed.
✓Does the merchant store full PANs or CVVs? Storing any SAD (Sensitive Authentication Data) after authorisation is a critical breach — escalate if confirmed.
✓Has a vulnerability scan been set up? SAQ D always requires an external ASV scan.
✅ Resolution Path
Complete the SAQ D questionnaire fully. Arrange scan if not already set up. Advise risk reduction measures (Decision Logic D). If profile results appear incorrect → escalate for review before proceeding. If merchant stores SAD (CVV/full PAN) after auth → this is a critical compliance issue — escalate to team lead immediately.
AG5 Account Closure Request 🚫 Closure ▼
📋 Situation
!A merchant contacts the helpdesk requesting to close or deactivate their PCI compliance account, typically because they have stopped accepting card payments or are closing their business.
⚠️ Account closure is not typically handled directly by the PCI Helpdesk. This is a function of the acquiring bank or programme administrator. Confirm your internal escalation path before taking any action.
⚙️ Steps to Take
1Authenticate the merchant fully — name, MID, business name, address, email.
2Ask why they are requesting closure: "Could I ask — are you closing the business, or have you stopped accepting card payments?"
3Check the account on the portal — note current compliance status, SAQ type, and whether any compliance steps are pending.
4Inform the merchant that account closure is managed by their acquiring bank or card scheme programme. Provide the correct contact route for the closure request.
5If applicable, advise the merchant about any outstanding compliance tasks and the potential impact if not completed prior to closure (e.g. ongoing non-compliance charges).
6Log the request in Zendesk. Note: MID, merchant name, reason for closure, current compliance status, and the information provided.
🔍 Key Checks
✓Is the person requesting closure authorised to do so? (Must be verified account holder.)
✓Are there outstanding non-compliance charges or compliance tasks that could result in additional charges if not resolved before closure?
✓Has the merchant been told the correct channel for the actual closure request? (Acquiring bank / programme admin — not the PCI Helpdesk.)
✅ Resolution Path
Acknowledge the request, check for outstanding tasks, inform the merchant of the correct closure channel (acquiring bank), note any compliance implications, and log the interaction in Zendesk. Do not process the closure yourself. If unclear on the correct escalation path → check with your team lead before advising.
AG6 Merchant Received Incorrect SAQ After Profiling 🔄 SAQ Mismatch ▼
📋 Situation
!The merchant has completed the business profile but the SAQ type generated by the portal does not match their actual payment environment. The merchant either flags this themselves, or the agent identifies the mismatch when reviewing the profile.
⚙️ What to Check First
1Review the merchant's profile answers on the portal carefully. Identify which profile question(s) may have been answered incorrectly or ambiguously.
2Ask the merchant the Payment Method Discovery question (Decision Logic B): "Can you describe exactly how your customers make card payments to you?"
3Cross-reference their answer against the SAQ type currently shown on the portal (use the SAQ routing table in Decision Logic B or the Quick Ref Script Phase 5).
✓Common mismatches: MOTO merchants profiled as face-to-face → SAQ B instead of C-VT. eCommerce redirect profiled as direct-post → SAQ A-EP instead of SAQ A.
⚙️ Resolution Decision Tree
PROFILE ERROR — CAN FIX
If you identify a clear profile question was answered incorrectly and you have authorisation to re-profile:
1. Explain to the merchant what needs to change and why
2. Go to Business Profile → Manage → Re-profile → Start business profile
3. Walk through all questions again — do not skip or suggest answers
4. Confirm the new SAQ type with the merchant after re-profiling
COMPLEX / UNCERTAIN — ESCALATE
If the correct SAQ type is unclear, or if you do not have authorisation to change the SAQ type:
1. Do NOT proceed with the incorrect SAQ
2. Explain to the merchant that you are escalating for review
3. Log the details in Zendesk: MID, current SAQ type, suspected correct SAQ type, reason
4. Escalate to team lead or senior agent — do not resolve alone
MERCHANT BELIEVES IT IS WRONG
If the merchant insists the SAQ is wrong but the profile answers appear correct:
1. Explain how the SAQ was determined based on their profile
2. Ask them to clarify their payment method again using the Discovery Question
3. If their description truly doesn't match → offer to re-profile
4. If still disputed after re-profile → escalate for technical or compliance review
✅ Resolution Path
Best approach: Always re-profile rather than trying to manually override or skip to a specific SAQ. The profile is the source of truth. If re-profiling doesn't resolve the mismatch, escalate — never leave a merchant on an incorrect SAQ type, as this invalidates their compliance submission.
